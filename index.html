About This Study

Survey Design

The AI and Human Flourishing Survey was developed by the Advancing Humans with AI (AHA) Research Program at MIT Media Lab. Drawing on validated instruments — including the Harvard Human Flourishing Index and the Comprehensive Inventory of Thriving — the survey measures both baseline wellbeing and the perceived impact of AI tools across multiple dimensions of human flourishing.

Measurement Approach

For each flourishing dimension, respondents first rate their current state on a 0–10 scale, then indicate how AI tools have affected that dimension on a separate 7-point impact scale (from “made this much worse” to “made this much better”). This paired design allows us to visualize both where people stand and which direction AI is pulling.

Sample

This preliminary dataset includes approximately 1,000 responses. Participants were recruited through Prolific and represent a diverse cross-section of AI users across demographics, education levels, and usage patterns. Data collection is ongoing, and visualizations will update as the sample grows.

How to Read the Visualizations

Spectrum track — The gradient from red to green represents the full response scale, from negative to positive.
Current distribution — The light grey curve shows where baseline responses cluster. Taller peaks mean more people responded near that value.
AI-adjusted distribution — The dark grey curve shows where responses shift after accounting for perceived AI impact. Compare with the light curve to see the direction of change.
Average marker — The dashed vertical line marks the mean score. The “AI” label marks the AI-adjusted average. Hover for details.
